Understanding the Market
📈

Why do prices go up?

Fertilizer prices rise when demand is high (planting seasons), energy costs increase (gas is used to make ammonia/urea), or supply is disrupted by weather, sanctions, or export bans.

📉

Why do prices fall?

Prices drop when supply outpaces demand, energy costs fall, or major producers like China and Russia increase exports. Seasonal slowdowns after planting also push prices down.

🌍

FOB vs CFR vs CIF

FOB (Free On Board) means the buyer pays shipping. CFR (Cost and Freight) means the seller pays freight to the destination port. CIF also includes insurance. Prices differ by location and terms.

🌾

Why fertilizer matters

Fertilizers feed half the world's population. Urea, DAP, MOP, and Ammonia are the building blocks of modern farming. When prices are high, food production costs rise for farmers everywhere.

⚡

The energy connection

Ammonia and urea are made from natural gas. When gas prices spike (like in 2022 after the Ukraine war), fertilizer prices follow. This is the single biggest driver of urea prices globally.

🔄

Seasons and cycles

Prices typically rise before spring planting (Jan-Mar in the Northern Hemisphere) and fall after demand peaks. Watch for these seasonal patterns in the price charts above.

Key Terms Explained
Urea
The most widely used nitrogen fertilizer in the world. Made from ammonia and CO2. Applied directly to soil or dissolved in water.
DAP (Di-ammonium Phosphate)
A phosphate fertilizer that also contains nitrogen. One of the most widely used phosphate products globally.
MOP (Muriate of Potash)
The most common potassium fertilizer. Used to improve crop yield, drought resistance, and disease resistance.
Ammonia
The foundation of all nitrogen fertilizers. Made from natural gas and nitrogen from the air. Also used directly as fertilizer in some countries.
Spot Price
The current market price for immediate delivery. Reflects real-time supply and demand conditions.
USD/t
US Dollars per metric tonne. The standard unit for fertilizer pricing internationally. One tonne = 1,000 kilograms.
Black Sea
A major export hub for fertilizers from Russia and Ukraine. Black Sea prices are a key global benchmark for urea and ammonia.
Sulphur / Sulphuric Acid
Sulphur is a byproduct of oil refining used to make phosphate fertilizers. Sulphuric acid is the key input for producing DAP and MAP.
